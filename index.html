ACETAMINOPHEN

Comprehensive Drug Information

Introduction

Paracetamol (also known as acetaminophen) is a widely used analgesic and antipyretic drug. It is commonly used for the relief of mild to moderate pain and reduction of fever.

Chemical Formula

C8H9NO2

Structural Formula

Indications

Paracetamol is indicated for:

Headache Ear Pain Period Pain Post-Operative Pain Fever Reduction Post-vaccine Reaction Musculoskeletal Pain Gouty Arthritis

It is available both by prescription and over-the-counter (OTC) in various formulations.

Pharmacodynamics

Paracetamol inhibits prostaglandin synthesis in the CNS, with minimal peripheral anti-inflammatory effects. It acts primarily on COX-2 inhibition while having little effect on COX-1.

Pharmacokinetics

  • Absorption: 88% oral bioavailability
  • Distribution: Vd ~0.9L/Kg
  • Metabolism: Hepatic (glucuronidation, sulfation, CYP450)
  • Excretion: Renal (urine)
  • Half-life: ~2.5 hours

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity to paracetamol
  • Severe hepatic impairment

Pregnancy & Lactation

Pregnancy Category C - Use only if clearly needed. Generally considered safe in recommended doses during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

Side Effects

  • Hepatotoxicity (overdose)
  • Skin reactions
  • GI disturbances
  • Blood disorders (rare)

Important Warning

Do not exceed 4g (8 tablets) in 24 hours. Overdose can cause severe liver damage. Avoid alcohol while taking paracetamol.
